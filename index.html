    T-Shirt Printing

    30/3/2021

    This was the last activity of the 2 day EA Hands-on Session and the whole idea behind it was to create our own class tee's w/ custom prints for each person (besides the mandatory SP Engineering Academy logo). And hats off to the lecturer who thought of this idea, because it's probably favourite part of the 2 days.

    Now on to the actual process itself. Before we even started printing, we had to design our custom prints in Inkscape first. I finished this part pretty fast because I already knew what designs I wanted to print and had them on hand.

    So moving on, there're many different methods that you can use for T-shirt printing, all w/ there're own pros & cons. The method we used was Dye Sublimation.

    In detail, what we did was save our designs into .svg format for the printer to print onto a sheet of Sublimation paper. It should be noted that we had to print the design in mirror image. After that it was just a matter of using the heat press to press the sublimation paper down on the shirt to print the designs onto the shirt.

    And voila!
